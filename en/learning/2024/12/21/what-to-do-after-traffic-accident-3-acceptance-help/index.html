What to Do When You Get in a Traffic Accident? — Acceptance and Getting Help (3)

#Misc #Traffic Accident

Intro

In the previous post we covered what you can do after an accident, and I specifically called out “self-care” at the end — because the moment your emotions might really crash is when the “preliminary report” (Preliminary Traffic Accident Analysis Report) comes out…

Note
I’m not a professional. This post is based on my own situation and what I learned along the way. If there’s anything off, feel free to correct me. (Also note: this is based on the legal context in Taiwan.)

Applying for the Preliminary Report

Most folks probably know what the preliminary report is. When you get in an accident, you can apply for this document from the police. It’s based on the police’s review of the scene photos and both sides’ statements — in plain language, it’s the police’s preliminary analysis of the accident.

One important reminder here:

The preliminary report has no legal weight.

This is important.

Why? Because the preliminary report isn’t issued by a certified expert, so it’s reference only — no legal weight.

Does that make the preliminary report useless? No — it still has initial reference value, which is why people typically also go for a “Traffic Accident Assessment”. More on that later — back to the preliminary report.

The preliminary report can be applied for 30 days after the accident. You can apply via the police station listed on your Traffic Accident Party Registration Form, or online via the National Police Agency. If no one was injured, you can only apply via the original handling unit — online application is only for cases with injuries.

If applying online, prep your Citizen Digital Certificate so you can collect the document properly.

When applying, you can request three documents:

  • Scene diagram
  • Scene photos
  • Analysis report (i.e., the preliminary report)

The scene diagram and photos can actually be applied for 7 days after the accident, so you can grab those first while waiting the 30 days for the analysis report.

Sounds like the preliminary report isn’t useful? It actually does some initial clarification of liability, which lets you go file for mediation using it — giving you some baseline to negotiate with.

If you see the preliminary report and think the police’s call is off and want a deeper investigation into the cause, you apply for the “Traffic Accident Assessment”.

Applying for the Traffic Accident Assessment

In my case I wanted to dig deeper into the cause, so I paid out of pocket for the Traffic Accident Assessment. This is conducted by professional assessors — they review the scene, vehicle damage, scene photos/video, statements from each party, and produce a report.

This report has legal weight — it can be used as evidence in court.

Can you apply for the assessment before the preliminary report comes out? No — the assessment requires the preliminary report, so you have to wait.

There are several ways to apply for the assessment: through the police, the court, or the prosecutor’s office. In my case, I applied and paid out of pocket (NT$3,000).

Important — the assessment requires that you reported to the police, that the police handled the case, and that you apply within 6 months. If the case has entered the judicial process, you can no longer apply in your personal name — you have to apply through a formal written motion to the judge.

After submission, your application goes through review to determine acceptance — it’s not automatic.

What happens after applying? You wait for notice. After my application, it took about 30 days for a letter saying I needed to go to a designated location to review the surveillance footage from the accident day. This is so you can verify the scene is correctly represented, and you can raise objections to correct any errors.

After that, around 2-3 weeks later you get the assessment report.

Comparison between the preliminary report and the assessment:

Name Has legal weight? Self-paid? Notes
Preliminary Report (Preliminary Traffic Accident Analysis Report) X X Only records possible accident causes — initial determination by police, not formal experts. Doesn’t have legal weight and doesn’t record primary vs secondary cause
Traffic Accident Assessment Report O O Self-paid but has legal weight. Must wait for the preliminary report first. Records primary and secondary causes

Applying for mediation

Once you have the preliminary report, you can apply for mediation based on it — just go to your township/district office. It’s free.

Before mediation, prep these:

  • Preliminary report
  • Traffic Accident Party Registration Form
  • Other party’s contact info and call records
  • Insurance company notification record
  • Hospital medical records
  • Your own scene photos
  • Bank account info
  • Mandatory insurance certificate

Get all the evidence ready, just in case.

Also, have a sense of the amount you might need to pay. If anyone was injured, there’s almost always some amount involved — but the negotiation itself is a separate matter. I’d recommend talking to a legal professional for advice first.

Mediation and Traffic Accident Assessment can run in parallel — you can apply for mediation + assessment simultaneously. Do the first round of mediation first to understand the other party’s stance and let them know yours. Filing for mediation is for resolving the issue, not creating one.

If you read my earlier posts, I emphasized that regardless of fault, if anyone was injured you should reach out to the other party — show goodwill, and maybe they’ll reciprocate so it can be resolved more amicably. Whether they forgive you is their call — what matters is showing good faith.

After applying for mediation, you’ll get a notice in 2-3 weeks with a case number like “Mediation No. 1234”. This case number is used throughout — keep it safe.

Mediation reminders:

  • Mediation is a communication process, not an argument — stay calm
  • Bring all your evidence — the mediator sits between you both and decides based on evidence
  • Show up. If you don’t, the other party gets favorable evidence — this becomes part of the court record
  • Mentally prepare for the other party not showing up. Some people deliberately skip, which is their right — but it adds unfavorable evidence against them

In my own case, I went through mediation twice — both failed.

Get help and support

At this stage I’d strongly recommend you start seeking help if you haven’t already. After the preliminary report comes out, you can have lawyers or legal professionals do an initial analysis of the case.

Honestly — the moment the preliminary report comes out, if the other party was seriously injured, you may feel really down emotionally. At this point I’d strongly recommend paying attention to your mental state — there may be emotional issues, and a counselor can help.

You can find a counselor or psychologist through hospitals, social services bureaus, counseling centers, etc. Don’t let stigma get to you — seeing a counselor is a great way to relieve stress.

Letting professionals guide you and help you adjust your emotional state — that’s what lets you face what comes next.

Closing thoughts
