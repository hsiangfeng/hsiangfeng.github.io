Day 24 - Deploying to Zeabur

#Node.js #Node.js 30 Days Series

Intro

After getting the basic concept of servers in the previous post, of course we have to actually deploy a project to a server.

Zeabur

Zeabur is a PaaS platform. What’s PaaS? Platform as a Service.

A bit vague? Let me give an example. Say you want to build a house. What do you need? Foundation, cement, bricks, wood, etc. — not to mention designing the house itself. These are all things you need to do.

The PaaS concept is like hiring a professional construction company to build it for you. The company provides services like design, construction, materials, etc. — you just tell them what house you want and they build it. That’s the PaaS concept.

So in this concept, you’re the developer, the construction company is the PaaS platform, and the house is the project. You just focus on project development without worrying about server issues — that’s the benefit of PaaS.

There are many common PaaS platforms — for example, Heroku (which became paid earlier) is a PaaS platform. So we don’t have to worry too much about server issues — just register an account, create a project, deploy the project. Pretty convenient.

The core idea of PaaS platforms is abstracting away the complex server management work, so you can focus on project development. Really convenient for beginners.

Note
There’s also IaaS (Infrastructure as a Service) and SaaS (Software as a Service), but those aren’t what we’re covering here. Look them up if interested.

Why am I picking Zeabur? Because Zeabur is a Taiwanese PaaS platform — gotta support local platforms. Plus Zeabur offers a free plan ($5/month), and it’s really convenient. So let’s use Zeabur to deploy our project.
